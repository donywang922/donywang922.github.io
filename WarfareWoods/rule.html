Rules

Pieces

  • 1 Board (25x25in.)
  • D8 dice
  • 8 Unit pieces (5 assault troopers, one engineer, one sniper, one tank)

Board Setup

Two corners on either of the board are delegated as "starting zones" for the two players.

Each starting zone is a square of size "4 x 4", where each player can initially set-up their units in any position desired, as long as all of the units fit within the zone.

There are 3 capturable zones, which are of size "3 x 3", one at the center of the board, the other two are at the 2 corners that are not starting corners.

There are cells marked as "obstacles", which units cannot move or shoot through.

Unit Setup

There are 4 types of units: Tank, Mechanic, Sniper, and Assault. For each team, there are 5 Assault units, 1 Sniper unit, 1 Mechanic unit, and 1 Tank unit.

Units may be arranged in any order desired at the start of the game, as long as they fit within the 4x4 starting zone.

Sniper

  • 1 health point.
  • Can move 2 tiles, each tile the unit can move either up - down - left - right.
  • Shooting range of 8 tiles in eight cardinal (and intercardinal) directions.
  • Can only shoot once each turn, each shot deals 1 damage (taking away the shot enemy 1 health point)

Assault

  • 1 health point.
  • Can move 2 tiles (similar to Sniper).
  • Shooting range of 4 tiles, which means the Assault unit can shoot at any opponent unit if there is a path of length 4 tiles or shorter (i.e. 3 tiles, 2 tiles, …) between the opponent unit and the Assault unit.
  • Can shoot twice each turn, each shot must be landed on a different opponent unit. Each shot deals 1 damage.

Engineer

  • 1 health point.
  • Can move 2 tiles (similar to Sniper).
  • Shooting range of 4 tiles or shorter (similar to Assault)
  • Can shoot once each turn. Each shot deals 1 damage.
  • Has the ability to repair the tank unit for 1 health point if in direct contact to tank once each turn, at a cost of shooting in that turn.

Tank

  • 2 health points.
  • Can move 2 squares along or reverse the current direction, or alternatively turn (to the right, to the left, or backward to the current direction) and move one square.
  • Ability to shoot a ‘2x6’ beam in the current direction, and turning to a new direction cost a turn.
  • Has the ability to shoot a ‘2x6’ beam in the current direction, dealing 1 damage to all enemies caught in the beam.

Beginning of Game

Each player chooses a side to start on, and sets up their units accordingly in their zone.

A D8 is rolled to determine which player goes first, higher number begins the game.

Gameplay mechanic

A D8 is rolled to determine number of units a player is able to move in a turn. If the rolled number is higher than the number of units, then move all units.

In a turn, a unit can move to a new position and begin attack immediately.

Units can move up to their allowed tiles per turn, but may move less if desired.

Cover mechanic

For a unit U (which is either Sniper, Assault, Tank, or Mechanic) and an opponent unit O lies within U’s shooting range, if the straight line between the square of U and square of O crosses an obstacle’s square, then U can NOT shoot at O.

Winning condition

There are two modes of winning:

  • Capturing at least two of the capture zones for at least one turn. This is fulfilled by having at least one unit in the capture zone for a full turn, with no opposing units also present in the zone.
  • Eliminating all the other player’s units.

Power Up

There are 2 power ups on the board. The player can move over the power up to pick it up, and choose an ability, either:

  • Type 1 allows the player to choose any unit from the opposing team and deal one damage to it.
  • Type 2 allows the player to revive one lost unit, the revived unit is placed back at the player’s starting zone.

Once a power up is picked up, it can be stored and used by a player any time throughout the game. Once it is used once, it is to be removed from the game and cannot be used again.

Flowchart

Typical Game Length

In our play tests, each battle typically took 30 minutes, but a very careful play through could take 1-2 hours.

Trade-offs

Some trade-offs found in gameplay was the position of tanks vs the obstacles present on the board, as the limited movement of the tank and long barriers separating areas of the board caused you to usually be locked to your direction and your initial movements to be critical in the strategy you were implementing. Additionally, snipers needed to be very positioned deliberately on the board as their attack range and angle was different from other units, making their long range a trade-off with their limited angle of range. A major dilemma that was found was the blocking of shots from the barriers on the board, as it was not always clear when the unit was behind or not behind a barrier and therefore made aiming a difficulty and a dilemma between players

Game Dynamics

Throughout playtesting the game, several successful strategies emerged. Here are some of the more effective strategies which came up throughout the play test.
One strategy involved two simple ideas: send the tank down to the side zone with the log, and send all the geese/assault unit towards the center zone in a V formation. This strategy was quite effective in that you are positioning to capture two zones while also forcing your opponent to play at a much faster pace. Since the tank alongside a unit or two can control or at least contest a zone, much more units can focus the middle zone. This leads to very chaotic fights since players cannot always risk giving away the central zone so early into the game.
Another strategy that was seen throughout was a slower and focused strategy, more focused on setting up the board. In this case, the strategy involved moving the sniper unit towards the side with no log so the sniper can get a clear shot of the center and corner zone. From there, the player can choose to advance their units towards either of the flags, patiently leaving units in positions that are just out of range of the enemy units in order to react when the enemy units start taking action.
The final strategy to have emerged started the turn with splitting units evenly between each zone. When the opponent starts moving their units towards certain zones, the player will look to move all their units towards the two zones that have the least amount of opposing units. The opponent is put into an awkward position at times especially if the heavily committed towards certain locations and can lead to an early to mid-game advantage when pulled off.
